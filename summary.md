Fitness App Workout Dataset – Summary

Key Findings

1. Rising Engagement Over Time  
   Monthly workout counts show an overall upward trend across the analysis period, indicating growing member engagement and/or an expanding membership base.

2. Premium Members Are the Most Active  
   Premium members complete more workouts per person on average than Basic and Standard members, reflecting higher commitment and/or better utilization of their membership benefits.

3. Cardio Burns the Most Calories  
   Cardio sessions have the highest average calories burned, while Yoga and other low-intensity activities burn fewer calories per session. This matches expectations based on workout intensity.

4. Strong Relationship Between Duration and Calories  
   There is a clear positive relationship between workout duration and calories burned. Longer sessions generally result in more calories burned, although variation increases for long workouts.

5. Evening Workouts Dominate  
   Most workouts occur in the late afternoon and evening hours, with only a smaller portion of members exercising early in the morning. Premium members show relatively more off-peak daytime workouts than other tiers.

6. Member BMI Skews Toward Overweight/Obese Ranges  
   The BMI distribution suggests that many members start from overweight or moderately obese categories, which is consistent with a gym population aiming to lose weight and improve fitness.

Limitations

- The dataset is synthetic and generated programmatically, so real-world behavior may be more variable and influenced by factors not modeled here.
- Some cleaning decisions (e.g., imputing missing values with group medians) may simplify complex patterns that would require more sophisticated methods in real data.
- Satisfaction ratings and heart rate values are simulated; in reality, you would validate them against device logs or survey sources.

Possible Improvements

- Incorporate additional behavioral features, such as time since last visit, churn labels, or goal types (weight loss, muscle gain, etc.).
- Add revenue information (membership fees, personal training sessions) to connect workout behavior with financial performance.
- Use more advanced analytics such as clustering to segment members, or predictive models to forecast churn or upgrade likelihood.
- Collect and analyze equipment usage or class attendance data to optimize schedule and capacity planning.
